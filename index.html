Austine R. Oloo

I am a Computer Science PhD student at Binghamton University specializing in AI and Robotics. My research focuses on endowing robots with the ability to build a rich, semantic understanding of their surroundings, allowing them to navigate and manipulate objects effectively in complex 3D environments. To achieve this, I develop and integrate novel algorithms from computer vision, reinforcement learning, and Task and Motion Planning (TAMP).

My work aims to bridge the gap between high-level symbolic reasoning and low-level sensorimotor control. I am passionate about applying this research to enable more effective and intuitive Human-Robot Interaction, with the goal of creating robotic systems that can collaborate safely and naturally with people in shared workspaces. I am also actively exploring how modern foundation models can be grounded in robotic systems to serve as more powerful interfaces for perception and action.

I'm fortunate to be advised by Professor Shiqi Zhang.

Learning random things has been the greatest source of pleasure for me, and I hope this will never stop. Recently, I have been studying how large language models can enhance robotic decision-making and enable more natural human-robot communication in my free time.
